# Principles

## Own the outcome

The job is not to close a ticket. Understand the problem, make the decision, ship the result, and learn from what happens next.

## Make trade-offs explicit

Good engineering is judgment under constraints. Name the cost, the risk, and the reason before choosing a path.

## Reliability is product

Testing, accessibility, performance, and maintainability are not backstage concerns. Users experience all of them.

## Earn complexity

Start with the simplest system that explains the problem. Add machinery only when reality pays for it.

## Help the team think

Useful questions, clear language, and shared context scale further than individual heroics.

## Keep the room alive

Serious output does not require unnecessary seriousness. Energy is part of how great teams do hard things together.
